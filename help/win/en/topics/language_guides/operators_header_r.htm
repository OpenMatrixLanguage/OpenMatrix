Operators

Operators are used to combine operands.

Operators take one or more operands, although most take two. The behavior of an operator depends on the types of operands passed to it. The supported operators are described in this section. Full descriptions of each operator can be found in the Operator Descriptions (tables) section.

  • Arithmetic Operators
    Arithmetic operators include addition, subtraction, and multiplication, for example.
  • Inequality Operators
    Inequality operators include greater than, less than, or equal to, for example.
  • Logical Operators
    Logical operators include and, or, and not, for example.
  • Order of Precedence
    In mathematics, there is a predefined order in which operators are performed.
  • Special Operators
    Special operators include brackets, parentheses, and brackets, for example.
  • Keywords
    OML includes a collection of keywords and operators.
